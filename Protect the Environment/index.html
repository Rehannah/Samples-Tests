Save the Scarlet Ibis

National bird. Global effort.


The Scarlet Ibis is one of the national birds of the island of Trinidad and Tobago. It is prohibited from being hunted or stripped of its feathers. However, many ignorant hunters ignore the penalties of such actions and do so anyways. The bird's meat is a delicacy on the black market but fines for being caught can peak to TTD$100,000 (US$15,000) and up to 2 years in prison. Additionally, with the rise of climate change, the number of Scarlet Ibises that are seen is few, with only a few hundred in protected wildlife reserves, due to the increase in temperature in the already tropical region.

The Caroni Bird Sanctuary is a popular reserve for tourists and locals alike to come visit this majestic bird along with the island's other national treasure, the Cocrico. The Scarlet Ibis' beautiful red feathers makes it perfect to represent the nation of T&T whose national colours are red, white and black. The bird eats crabs which is where it gets its colour from and therefore is often seen in mangroves where crabs reside.
